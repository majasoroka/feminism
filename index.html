History Timeline

Key turning points, movements, and milestones in radical feminist history.

1848

Seneca Falls Convention

The first women's rights convention held in the United States, launching the organized women's suffrage movement and adopting the Declaration of Sentiments.

First Wave
1918

Representation of the People Act

In the UK, suffrage campaigners secured the right to vote for women over 30 who met property qualifications, marking a crucial step toward full voting equality in 1928.

Suffrage Movement
1968

Ford Sewing Machinists Strike

Women workers at the Ford plant in Dagenham, UK, went on strike for equal pay, directly inspiring the passage of the Equal Pay Act 1970.

Labor & Equal Pay
1970

First UK Women's Liberation Conference

Held at Ruskin College, Oxford, this landmark conference established core demands regarding equal pay, equal education, 24-hour nurseries, and reproductive autonomy.

Radical Feminism
1981

Greenham Common Women's Peace Camp

A major feminist environmental and anti-nuclear protest camp established in Berkshire, UK, demonstrating non-violent direct action and grassroots organizing.
